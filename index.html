Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

The chairman of Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) Madhavan Nair (L) gifts a moon model to former president of India A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (R) during a conference at the ISRO center in Bangalore on November 14, 2008.

Here's a time line of Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameshwaram, Madra Presidency, British India (present-day Tamil Nadu, India)
  • 1954 -Graduates in Physics Kalam graduates in physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli. He moves to Madras to pursue further education after graduation. "
  • 1960 - Joins the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist."
  • 1969 - Moves to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he helms India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1980 - Leads India's entry in the Space club by putting the Rohini satellite in Earth's orbit with the first indigenous SLV-III
  • 1980 - 1990 - Develops Prithvi and Agni missiles
  • 1981 - Recieves the Padma Bhushan Kalam for his contribution to the field of science and technology
  • 1990 - Honoured with Padma Vibhushan, India’s second highest civilian honour
  • 1992- 1999 - Appointed India’s Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the DRDO
  • 1997 - Honoured with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 - Spearheads the Pokhran II nuclear tests
  • 1999 - 2001 - Serves as India’s Chief Scientific Adviser to the Government of India
  • 2002 - 2007 - Elected as India's 11th President. He won the 2002 presidential election with an electoral vote of 922,884. During his five years in office, he was fondly called ‘The People’s President'
  • 2012 - Launches 'What Can I Give Movement, a programme for the Indian youth aimed at defeating corruption"
  • 2015 - Passes away in Shillong, Meghalaya at the age of 83, after collapsing because of a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong
